FACT CHECK - FALSE: The Indian government has NOT announced any scheme giving Rs 15 lakh to every citizen's bank account. This is a recurring viral hoax spread via WhatsApp and social media. The government's PIB Fact Check unit regularly debunks such claims on their official Twitter handle @PIBFactCheck. Always verify any financial claim on pib.gov.in before sharing.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: There is no nationwide government scheme offering free smartphones to all citizens of India. Some state governments have specific schemes such as Rajasthan's Free Smartphone Yojana for women under the Indira Gandhi Smartphone Yojana (IGSY), but there is no all-India free smartphone distribution. Verify on official state government websites.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: WhatsApp messages claiming that the government will demonetise old Rs 500 and Rs 2000 notes again are FALSE. The Rs 2000 note was withdrawn from circulation in May 2023 but remained legal tender for exchange at bank branches until October 7, 2023. No new demonetisation has been announced. Always check RBI's official website rbi.org.in.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Viral messages claiming government will give free laptops to all students under a PM Laptop Yojana are FALSE. No such central government scheme exists. State-level schemes like Tamil Nadu's laptop scheme exist but are not national schemes. Verify any educational scheme on education.gov.in.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Messages claiming Aadhaar card will be cancelled or deactivated if not linked with a particular bank account or phone number within 24-48 hours are FAKE. UIDAI does not send such messages. Aadhaar linking deadlines, if any, are announced officially on uidai.gov.in and through official press releases.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Claims that the government is giving Rs 1 lakh to all BPL card holders or ration card holders through a new scheme are FALSE. No such scheme exists. Such messages are designed to collect personal data or commit fraud. Never share your Aadhaar, bank account, or OTP with anyone claiming to be a government official via social media.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: A viral message claiming COVID-19 vaccines cause infertility, autism, or genetic changes is FALSE and has been debunked by WHO, ICMR, and global health authorities. India's approved vaccines (Covishield, Covaxin, etc.) went through rigorous clinical trials. Vaccine misinformation is dangerous and spreads to cause vaccine hesitancy.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Messages claiming that a new law has been passed making all property sales illegal or requiring government permission for every home sale are completely FALSE. No such law has been passed in India. Property registration procedures are governed by the Registration Act, 1908. Verify legal changes on indiacode.nic.in.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Viral claims that the government is providing free electricity of 300 units per month to all households are misleading. The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana provides up to 300 units free to households that install rooftop solar panels of 1-3 kW capacity, not to everyone automatically. Registration is required at pmsuryaghar.gov.in.

FACT CHECK - FALSE: Messages claiming a new UPI tax or 18% GST has been imposed on all UPI transactions are FALSE. UPI payments for personal use between individuals (P2P) remain free of charge. No GST applies to UPI transaction amounts. Any such claims are misinformation aimed at discouraging digital payments.

FACT CHECK - VERIFIED TRUE: The PM Vishwakarma Yojana is a real scheme launched in 2023 providing recognition and support to traditional artisans and craftspeople (vishwakarmas). Benefits include recognition via PM Vishwakarma certificate, skill training with stipend of Rs 500 per day, a toolkit incentive of Rs 15,000, and collateral-free credit up to Rs 3 lakh at 5% interest.

FACT CHECK - VERIFIED TRUE: The Poshan Abhiyaan (National Nutrition Mission) is a real government scheme targeting malnutrition in children under 6 years, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. It uses a mobile technology-based real-time monitoring system. The scheme aims to reduce stunting, wasting, and underweight prevalence by 2% each year.
