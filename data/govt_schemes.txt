Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) provides health insurance cover of Rs 5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. It covers over 10.74 crore poor and vulnerable families. The scheme is cashless and paperless at public and empanelled private hospitals. Beneficiaries do not need to pay anything at the hospital.

PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi) provides income support of Rs 6,000 per year in three equal instalments of Rs 2,000 each to all landholding farmer families. The amount is directly transferred to the bank accounts of the beneficiaries. Over 11 crore farmers benefit from this scheme across India.

MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act) guarantees 100 days of wage employment per financial year to every rural household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. The current wage rate varies by state, ranging from Rs 220 to Rs 333 per day. Workers must be paid within 15 days of work completion.

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana provides free LPG connections to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households. Under Ujjwala 2.0, migrant workers can also get connections with a self-declaration. Over 9.6 crore LPG connections have been released under this scheme. The scheme aims to replace unclean cooking fuels.

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) aims to provide affordable housing to the urban and rural poor. Under PMAY-Gramin, the central assistance is Rs 1.20 lakh in plain areas and Rs 1.30 lakh in hilly or difficult terrain areas. Under PMAY-Urban, the credit-linked subsidy ranges from Rs 2.35 lakh to Rs 2.67 lakh depending on the income category.

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a national financial inclusion program ensuring every unbanked adult gets a zero-balance savings bank account, a RuPay debit card, and Rs 2 lakh accident insurance cover. Over 50 crore accounts have been opened under this scheme. The scheme also provides access to credit, insurance, and pension.

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a small-savings scheme for the girl child under the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao initiative. It offers an interest rate of 8.2% per annum (as of 2024). A minimum deposit of Rs 250 and a maximum of Rs 1.5 lakh per year is allowed. The account matures after 21 years from the date of opening or on the girl's marriage after age 18.

Under the National Food Security Act (NFSA) and Public Distribution System (PDS), Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) families receive 35 kg of food grains per month. Priority Household (PHH) ration card holders receive 5 kg per person per month at highly subsidised rates of Rs 1-3 per kg. The One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) scheme allows migrants to use their ration card anywhere in India.

Digital India programme aims to transform India into a digitally empowered society and knowledge economy. Key initiatives include DigiLocker for secure document storage, UMANG app for government services, BharatNet for rural broadband connectivity, and Common Service Centres (CSCs) for digital access in villages. Over 1 lakh CSCs operate across rural India.

Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) provides collateral-free loans up to Rs 10 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises. Three categories: Shishu loans up to Rs 50,000 for startups, Kishore loans from Rs 50,001 to Rs 5 lakh for existing businesses, and Tarun loans from Rs 5,00,001 to Rs 10 lakh for larger expansions.

Swachh Bharat Mission aims to achieve universal sanitation coverage and eliminate open defecation. Under SBM-Gramin, a financial incentive of Rs 12,000 is provided for construction of individual household latrines. Over 11 crore toilets have been built since the scheme's launch in 2014. India was declared Open Defecation Free (ODF) in 2019.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) provides comprehensive crop insurance to farmers against crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. Premium rates are very low: 2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops, and 5% for commercial and horticultural crops. The remaining premium is shared equally between Central and State governments.

Skill India Mission (PMKVY - Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana 4.0) provides free skill training and industry-recognised certification. Training covers over 300 job roles across 40 sectors. Trainees receive a monetary reward of Rs 8,000 on average upon successful certification. The scheme focuses on new-age skills including AI, robotics, and coding.

Atal Pension Yojana (APY) is a government-backed pension scheme for unorganised sector workers. Subscribers aged 18-40 years can contribute monthly to receive a guaranteed pension of Rs 1,000 to Rs 5,000 per month after age 60. The government co-contributes 50% of the subscriber's contribution or Rs 1,000 per year (whichever is lower) for eligible subscribers.

Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) provides life insurance cover of Rs 2 lakh for death due to any reason at an annual premium of just Rs 436. Any bank account holder between 18 and 50 years can enrol. The scheme is renewed annually with auto-debit from the subscriber's bank account.

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) provides accidental death and disability insurance of Rs 2 lakh at a very low annual premium of Rs 20. Available to bank account holders between 18 and 70 years. In case of partial disability, Rs 1 lakh is paid. The scheme is auto-renewed each year.

Stand-Up India scheme facilitates bank loans between Rs 10 lakh and Rs 1 crore to at least one SC/ST borrower and at least one woman borrower per bank branch for setting up greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors. The loan is repayable in 7 years with a maximum moratorium period of 18 months.
